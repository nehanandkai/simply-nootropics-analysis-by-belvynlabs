How Simply Nootropics Started
Burnout, bad supplements, and a better idea

Anthony Baxter wasn't looking to build a supplement brand. He was running Firefly Digital, a performance marketing and eCommerce agency in New Zealand, constantly travelling between Auckland and Sydney, burning the candle at both ends. The burnout hit hard. He discovered nootropics — cognitive performance supplements — and found that the right compounds genuinely helped. The problem was that everything available either underdosed the active ingredients, used cheap fillers, or came with no real scientific credibility behind the formulation.

He teamed up with Stuart Vaughan, a natural health expert with deep knowledge of the supplement supply chain, and launched Simply Nootropics in 2021. The timing was deliberate — the longevity and biohacking conversation was growing fast in the US and UK, but Australia and New Zealand were significantly behind. Anthony saw the gap and moved into it early.

The credential that changed everything came when they brought Dr. Brian P. Ramos onto the team — a Yale-trained neuroscientist with a PhD in Neurobiology. Having an actual neuroscientist formulate the products and publicly endorse them gave Simply Nootropics a level of scientific credibility that almost no brand at their size in the ANZ market could match. Anthony announced the hire publicly on LinkedIn and positioned it as assembling "the Avengers of Health."

What's particularly interesting is Anthony's background. He built and sold Firefly Digital — which means he understands eCommerce, paid social, and creative performance better than most supplement founders. He knows what good advertising looks like. Which makes the creative gap in their current paid social output even more notable — he has the knowledge, but clearly doesn't have the production volume to act on it.

The brand has now expanded beyond New Zealand and Australia into the US market, with a Delaware-registered US entity and a second brand — Tmrw (drinktmrw.com) — a premium daily longevity drink. They're also actively hiring a Creative Strategy Lead to own paid social across both brands. That job listing is the most honest signal you'll find about where their current gap is.

02
The Products & Range
10+ SKUs anchored by NMN powder — with a growing longevity and cognitive performance stack
Hero Product ★

Ageless NMN Powder

Pure NMN powder — no fillers, tasteless, mixes easily in water. Dosed at 500mg–1000mg depending on age. Third-party tested in Australia. 60-day money-back guarantee. Priced at ~$0.70/serving on the 3-month supply. The product that built the brand.

Methylation Support

TMG Powder

Trimethylglycine — pairs with NMN to support methylation and prevent homocysteine buildup from increased NAD+ production. Sold as a duo with NMN. Most customers take both together. This bundling is smart — it increases cart value and LTV.

Cognitive Performance

Simply Nootropics Essentials

Multi-ingredient nootropic capsule stack — Lion's Mane, NZ Pine Bark, Ashwagandha, L-Theanine. Focus, memory, stress resilience. The entry point for people who aren't yet ready for NMN.

Collagen & Beauty

Vital Beauty Collagen

Marine collagen powder targeting skin, hair, nails. Expands the brand into the beauty-longevity crossover market. Several strong reviews specifically on this product — skin firmness and nail strength mentioned repeatedly.

Sleep

Genius Sleep

Sleep-focused formula — relaxation, sleep onset, and quality. "Best sleep product I have tried so far, without any side effects" — multiple reviews say this. Strong secondary SKU.

New Brand

Tmrw (drinktmrw.com)

Premium longevity daily drink — NAD+ support, gut health, cellular health. Separate brand but same team. Suggests Anthony is building a portfolio play, not just a single brand.

The NMN positioning is smart but carries a legal wrinkle: NMN was banned from sale as a supplement in the US by the FDA in November 2022. Simply Nootropics gets around this by shipping from New Zealand/Australia under the personal importation scheme — customers can legally import a 90-day supply for personal use. They do not directly market NMN to the US public via paid ads or Amazon to comply with this. This limits their US paid social reach on the core NMN product, which makes the Tmrw brand — which uses NR instead — their primary US growth vehicle.

What's Worth Paying Attention To
Five things that stood out — the gap between what the brand has and what it's doing with it
🎯

They're hiring a Creative Strategy Lead — which means they don't have one yet

Simply Nootropics posted a job for a Creative Strategy Lead to own paid social creative across both Simply Nootropics and Tmrw. The job description says creative performance "directly impacts revenue" and that they need someone to "develop strategic, performance-driven UGC briefs across products and markets." That's not a nice-to-have hire — that's a brand that knows it has a creative problem and is trying to solve it. The hire will take months to find, onboard, and produce. That window is the opportunity.

📝

600+ reviews contain the best ad copy they've never used

"My brain fog is gone." "Stopped needing an afternoon nap." "My doctor asked what I changed." "3 months on NMN and TMG and I'm a different person." These are verbatim customer quotes sitting in Trustpilot right now. They're more compelling than anything a copywriter will invent because they're true and specific. A systematic approach to turning the best review language into UGC hooks — at volume — would outperform any polished brand video they currently run. The raw material is already there. It just needs to be built into creative.

🧪

Anthony's burnout story is the most powerful hook they're not using at scale

Anthony Baxter built and sold a digital agency. He burned out. He discovered nootropics. He was so frustrated by the quality of existing products that he built a better one. He then hired a Yale neuroscientist to formulate it. That's a complete, compelling, founder-led story that the target customer — the burned-out professional — would find completely relatable. It's currently buried in an About page. It should be the opening frame of every cold audience video ad they run. Anthony understands paid social better than most supplement founders — which means he'll recognise immediately why this approach works.

🌏

Two brands, two markets, one creative team — the maths doesn't add up

Simply Nootropics is running paid social in Australia, New Zealand, and now the US via Tmrw. That's three markets across two brands with what appears to be a very small creative team — hence the Creative Strategy Lead hire. The volume of creative needed to test properly across those markets — different hooks, different audience segments, different funnel stages — is significant. A brand trying to scale in three markets with limited creative output will inevitably hit a ceiling on paid social performance, regardless of how good the product is.

🔬

Dr. Ramos is an underused creative asset

Having a Yale neuroscientist on the team is extraordinary for a brand at Simply Nootropics' stage. But looking at the creative output, Dr. Ramos appears primarily on the website's About page and in a LinkedIn announcement. He's not in the ads. He's not explaining the science in short-form video. He's not the face of the product's credibility in paid social. A neuroscientist saying "here's what actually happens to your brain when you take NMN" — in plain language, to camera, for 30 seconds — is a category-defining ad format that nobody else in the ANZ market is running.
